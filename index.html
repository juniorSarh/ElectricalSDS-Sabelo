OSHA Electrical Safety SDS

Based on OSHA Standard 1926.431

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

  • Head Protection
    • Prevents head injuries from falling objects, confined spaces, and electrical work.
    • Provide safety helmets or hard hats.
    • Keep spare helmets for visitors and to replace damaged units.
  • Eye Protection
    • Shields eyes from dust, chemicals, debris, and welding sparks.
    • Use safety goggles or over-glasses for those who wear spectacles.
  • Hand Protection
    • Protects hands from chemicals, burns, cuts, and machinery injuries.
    • Options include:
      • Latex gloves for delicate tasks
      • Heavy-duty gloves for tasks requiring less dexterity
  • Foot Protection
    • Prevents injury from heavy/sharp objects, corrosive substances, electric shock, and falls.
    • Types include:
      • Steel-capped boots
      • Anti-slip footwear
      • Gum boots
  • Hearing Protection
    • Guards against hearing loss from loud, sustained noise.
    • Devices include:
      • Ear plugs (inserted into ear canal)
      • Ear muffs (over-ear, less protective and may cause discomfort)
  • Face Protection
    • Protects against chemicals, molten metal, and impact hazards.
    • Recommended gear includes:
      • Brow guards
      • Face visors
  • Respiratory Protection
    • Prevents inhalation of harmful dust, fumes, solvents, and chemicals.
    • Options include:
      • Disposable respirators
      • Half- and full-face masks
      • Respiratory helmets
